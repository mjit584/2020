Making Chocolate the Mayan Way

Chocolate originated in the Yucatan Peninsula and has played an integral role in Mayan social, economic and religious life for over 2000 years. Today, Mayan communities in Belize continue to grow and process chocolate using a combination of traditional and modern methods. Take a look at this step-by-step guide to see how chocolate gets from the cacao farm to the grocery store.

Roasting

Roasted beans are transferred to a stone grinder called a metate. Using a stone rolling pin, chocolate makers break beansâ€™ shells and expose cocoa nibs, or tiny shards of chocolate.

Winnowing

In order to sift extraneous pieces of shell from cocoa nibs, chocolate-makers transfer contents from the metate to a large metal pan. In a process called winnowing, chocolate-makers toss the mixture in the air, allowing wind to catch and blow away lighter shells while heavier nibs fall back into the pan. This process continues until no shells remain.
